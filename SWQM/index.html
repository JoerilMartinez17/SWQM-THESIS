About Our Study

Smart Water Quality Management for Tank-Based Cream Dory Farming, was developed to address the challenges faced by many local fish farmers who still rely on manual methods of checking and maintaining water conditions. Inconsistent monitoring can lead to poor water quality, fish stress, and reduced production efficiency. Recognizing this, our team sought to create a system that combines technology and sustainability to make aquaculture smarter and more reliable.

Beyond just automation, this study aims to empower local Cream Dory (Pangasius hypophthalmus) farmers with an innovative tool that minimizes manual labor, prevents water-related problems, and enhances fish growth and survival rates. In the long run, this project promotes sustainable aquaculture practices that can contribute to the development of the Philippine Pangasius industry, helping it grow toward local food security and global export potential.

The system uses Arduino, ESP32, and Recirculating Aquaculture System (RAS) technology to automatically monitor key water parameters such as pH, temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, ammonia, and water level in real time. By providing accurate and continuous readings, the system helps farmers ensure that their fish are raised in healthy and stable water conditions.
